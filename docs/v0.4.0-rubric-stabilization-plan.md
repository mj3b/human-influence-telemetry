# Human Influence Telemetry v0.4.0 Rubric Stabilization Plan

**Status:** Active development plan  
**Branch:** `develop/hit-v0.4.0-rubric-stabilization`  
**Candidate normative source:** `docs/normative-decision-rules-v0.4.0-draft.md`  
**Research maturity:** Level 1, Defined  
**Human reliability claim:** Unresolved

## 1. Release objective

Version `0.4.0` will convert the ambiguity classes recorded in `HIT-ARFR-001` into a synchronized, testable assessment contract.

The release is a normative-method update. It does not establish human inter-rater reliability, field effectiveness, legal compliance, certification, or independent adoption.

## 2. Contract changes under review

The candidate rules introduce:

- an evidence-state model separating affirmative absence, formal presence, operational capability, and observed exercise;
- explicit thresholds for `0`, `1`, `2`, and `IE`;
- dimension-specific rules for Counsel, Judgment, Command, Correction, Repair, and Reform;
- separate institutional-record and assessment-packet integrity statuses;
- deterministic derivation of the overall Telemetry Integrity status;
- a dominant-pattern default for period-level aggregation;
- a required actor-authority matrix;
- source-conflict weighting rules;
- construct-specific rules for cross-dimensional evidence reuse;
- precise citation locators and bounded evidence propositions.

Files:

Completion test: the schema rejects records that omit the new normative information and preserves the six-plus-one construct model.

### WP-D: Executable fixtures

Required fixture classes:

1. explicit absence versus silence;
2. formal role without process applicability;
3. formal presence with no operational capability;
4. tested operational capability without observed exercise;
5. actual Counsel access versus general permission;
6. Judgment based on direct reasons versus indirect indicators;
7. Command held by a vendor rather than the assessed actor;
8. Correction channel intake without substantive reconsideration;
9. triggered, not-triggered, and indeterminate Repair states;
10. later Reform with and without documented linkage;
11. adequate, limited, unreliable, and `IE` integrity components;
12. rare exercise versus dominant pattern;
13. separated versus improperly combined actors;
14. resolvable versus material contradictory evidence;
15. valid versus invalid cross-dimensional evidence reuse;
16. precise versus non-reproducible citation locators.

Each class requires at least one accepted and one rejected or differently classified fixture.

Completion test: expected outcomes are deterministic and the validator rejects every known invalid state.

### WP-E: Case migration

Files:

- public case narratives;

Tasks:

1. preserve all released `0.1.0` assessments as historical artifacts;
2. produce candidate `0.4.0` migrations separately;

4. do not silently overwrite historical findings;
5. identify cases that cannot migrate without additional evidence.

Completion test: every public case either validates under `0.4.0` or has an explicit migration exception.

## 5. Protected artifacts

The following must not change as part of this workstream:

- original scorer submissions, when collected;
- `HIT-IRP-CIGNA-001` protocol terms;
- frozen Cigna packet contents and source hashes;
- pre-adjudication comparison threshold;
- critical-disagreement definitions;
- historical public assessments released under specification `0.1.0`;
- the statement that H3 and Maturity Level 2 remain unresolved.

## 6. Stop conditions

Do not publish `v0.4.0` when any of the following remains true:

- a friction class lacks a governing rule;
- the specification and handbook conflict;
- the schema cannot represent a required rule;
- a boundary fixture has no deterministic expected outcome;
- historical and migrated case records are not clearly separated;
- the validator accepts a known invalid state;
- release language implies human reliability, legal conformity, certification, or independent adoption;
- the locked protocol or historical findings are altered without an explicit separate decision.

## 7. Sequence

1. review candidate normative rules;
2. incorporate approved rules into specification and handbook;
3. design schema changes;
4. add boundary fixtures and validator rules;
5. migrate public cases;
6. publish breaking-change and migration reports;
7. run clean validation;
8. prepare the `v0.4.0` release candidate.
