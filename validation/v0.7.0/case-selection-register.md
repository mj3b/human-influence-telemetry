# HIT 0.4.0 Replication Case-selection Register

**Protocol:** `HIT-IRP-HIT040-002`  
**Status:** Candidate screening  
**Scorer access:** Coordinator-only until packets are locked

## Selection objective

The first human exercise produced complete agreement and no category variance. The next sample must test whether the current contract distinguishes different evidence states and findings.

Case selection uses observable source-surface properties. It does not use hidden expected scores.

## Required strata

### Slot A: exercise-rich

The frozen public record must contain direct evidence that a named human authority used counsel, judgment, command, correction, repair, or reform in a way that changed a decision path.

Minimum source-surface conditions:

- one attributable human action;
- one identifiable decision consequence;
- one reproducible locator;
- one source independent of the assessed institution, or one adjudicated or procedurally tested institutional record;
- enough actor and timing information to evaluate attribution.

### Slot B: constraint-rich

The record must establish a process-specific human role and at least two mutually reinforcing indicators that substantive exercise was constrained.

Eligible indicators include:

- review duration incompatible with the assigned task;
- batch processing;
- boilerplate reasons;
- near-total system-output adoption;
- unavailable underlying evidence;
- override friction;
- another actor controlling the intervention mechanism;
- appeal intake without substantive reconsideration.

### Slot C: evidence-limited

A documented source search must leave material propositions unresolved. The packet must contain enough information to define the process and actors while preserving genuine uncertainty on at least two dimensions.

The unresolved state must arise from evidence limits. A coordinator may not manufacture `IE` by excluding a readily available source that would resolve the proposition.

## Cross-sample evidence-state coverage

The combined three-packet source surface must contain all four tests below before lock:

1. an explicit nonexistence statement, structural impossibility, credibly complete search, or contemporaneous exclusion capable of testing affirmative absence;
2. a process-specific role, mechanism, step, or channel capable of testing formal presence;
3. a documented human action with an identifiable decision consequence capable of testing observed exercise;
4. a documented search or request that leaves a material proposition unresolved and capable of testing indeterminate evidence.

These are packet-selection conditions. They do not assign findings. Independent scorers determine whether the source artifacts satisfy the contract.

## General inclusion criteria

Every selected case must have:

1. a consequential institutional decision process involving an AI, algorithmic, or automated decision-support system;
2. a bounded institutional unit and period or event;
3. a public source surface that can be frozen and archived lawfully;
4. at least two independent record classes or a competent adjudicated record;
5. enough information to construct a stable actor roster;
6. reproducible locators;
7. no need for confidential author explanation;
8. manageable packet length for three independent scorers;
9. a defensible Repair-trigger procedure;
10. a case-specific reason the packet adds discriminating evidence to the study.

## Exclusion criteria

Exclude a case when:

- the system's role cannot be distinguished from ordinary software;
- the unit of analysis shifts across sources;
- the public record consists only of allegations with no corroborating record class;
- source access depends on unstable summaries that cannot be archived;
- scorer exposure to prior HIT findings creates material contamination that cannot be controlled;
- one institution is treated as holding another institution's authority without evidence;
- the packet would require legal conclusions to assign HIT findings;
- the likely evidence profile duplicates another selected packet without adding a distinct test.

## Selection procedure

1. Identify at least three candidates for each slot.
2. Build a source-function inventory for every candidate.
3. Record what each source can support and what it cannot support.
4. Test whether the unit, period, actor roster, sampling rule, and Repair trigger can be fixed before scoring.
5. Conduct a contamination review against public HIT materials and known scorer exposure.
6. Confirm that the combined set covers all four evidence-state tests.
7. Select one candidate per slot.
8. Publish the selected-case rationale without publishing expected findings.
9. Freeze the decision boundary, source manifest, source archive, packet digest, and exclusion list.
10. Update the protocol lock with three packet IDs.
11. Run exact-commit validation before recruitment.

## Decision rule

The selected set must contain credible source surfaces for affirmative absence, formal presence, observed exercise, and indeterminate evidence across the 18 substantive dimension units. The coordinator documents the source basis for this coverage. The record must avoid assigning target scores before independent review.
