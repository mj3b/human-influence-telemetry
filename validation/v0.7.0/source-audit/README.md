## Purpose

This directory converts the initial prose screening record into a traceable source-function audit for the six highest-ranked candidates. It does not select cases, assign packet IDs, predict HIT findings, or interpret evidence for a scorer.

The protocol prohibits generative-model assistance for substantive source selection, evidence interpretation, finding assignment, rationale drafting, and ambiguity analysis. Automation may organize already-recorded candidate metadata, validate required fields, check file structure, and preserve decision provenance. A named human coordinator must make and sign the final selection decision.

## Required human audit

For each candidate, the human coordinator must review the actual source surface and record:

1. exact artifact title and version;
2. publisher or issuing authority;
3. publication or filing date;
4. source function;
5. procedural posture;
6. stable locator quality;
7. archive and redistribution status;
8. actor and authority information;
9. Repair-trigger evidence availability;
10. evidence-state test coverage;
11. packet burden;
12. contamination risk;
13. inclusion or exclusion decision;
14. signed rationale and date.

## Prohibited content

Candidate records must not contain:

- expected dimension findings;
- predicted `0`, `1`, `2`, or `IE` outcomes;
- scorer-facing stratum labels;
- author findings;
- a recommendation generated by a language model;
- a packet ID before the human selection record is signed;
- evidence interpretation written for later scorer use.

## Selection gate

Exactly one candidate must be selected for each slot. The human coordinator records the decision in `human-selection-decision.md`.

A selected candidate may receive a packet ID only after:

- its source-function inventory is complete;
- archive and redistribution constraints are resolved;
- the decision boundary can be fixed;
- the actor roster is stable;
- the Repair-trigger procedure is defensible;
- contamination risk is accepted explicitly;
- the final selection is signed and dated.

## Next stage

Protocol status remains `candidate`, and scoring remains prohibited until the final packet digests, comparison implementation, synthetic vectors, instructions, and exact-commit validation are complete.
