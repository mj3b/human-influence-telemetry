# HIT Current-Contract Replication Protocol Candidate

**Protocol ID:** `HIT-IRP-MULTICASE-002`  
**Protocol version:** `0.1.0-candidate`  
**Target repository release:** `v0.7.0`  
**Method under test:** HIT specification, schema, catalog, and handbook `0.4.0`  
**Conformance engine:** `0.5.0`  
**Status:** Candidate. Scoring prohibited.

## 1. Research question

Can independent reviewers apply the public HIT `0.4.0` contract to three frozen public-record packets without private author explanation and produce findings that satisfy predeclared agreement, discrimination, and evidence-traceability rules?

The study tests reproducibility across multiple cases and evidence conditions. It does not test causal effectiveness, legal correctness, evidence truth, field effectiveness, certification, or institutional adoption.

## 2. Study design

The study uses three frozen case packets. Each packet represents one bounded institutional decision architecture and one declared actor boundary.

The case set must include:

1. a packet expected to contain evidence of substantive human exercise on at least one dimension;
2. a packet expected to contain formal or ceremonial human presence on at least one dimension;
3. a packet expected to leave at least one material dimension at `IE`.

These are sampling strata, not author-assigned findings. Scorers do not receive expected categories.

At least three eligible independent scorers participate. Every packet receives at least two independent scores. Scorer allocation must prevent one scorer pair from determining every packet result when three or more scorers are available.

## 3. Unit of analysis

Each packet fixes:

- institutional unit;
- decision process;
- actor or actor class;
- assessment period;
- affected population or record universe;
- sampling and aggregation rule;
- source boundary;
- procedural posture of each source;
- Repair trigger state to be evaluated;
- known exclusions.

A scorer evaluates only the declared unit. Findings may not be generalized beyond the packet boundary.

## 4. Eligibility and independence

Each scorer must:

1. be someone other than the HIT author;
2. have made no material contribution to the `0.4.0` contract, selected case findings, packet construction, or this protocol;
3. be able to evaluate governance, assurance, legal, policy, audit, or socio-technical evidence with professional competence;
4. disclose financial, organizational, litigation, advocacy, or personal conflicts relevant to any packet;
5. attest that they did not inspect author-scored records, coordinator expected findings, or another scorer's work;
6. complete the work without interpretive assistance from the author or another scorer;
7. use only the frozen materials supplied for the assigned packet.

A false, incomplete, or contradicted independence attestation excludes the submission from the primary analysis. The excluded file remains preserved.

## 5. Blinding and coordinator controls

Scorers receive:

- the public `0.4.0` specification;
- the public `0.4.0` application handbook;
- the frozen packet assigned to them;
- the scorer-submission schema and completion instructions;
- technical instructions for running conformance checks.

The coordinator may resolve file-access, schema, and execution problems. The coordinator may not interpret evidence, recommend a finding, identify an expected category, reveal another score, or explain how the author would apply a threshold.

Scorers submit separately. Original files are preserved before transcription, validation, comparison, or discussion. Clerical corrections require a new file, a recorded reason, and preservation of the superseded file.

## 6. Scoring procedure

Each scorer independently:

1. reads the `0.4.0` specification and handbook;
2. reviews every required frozen source;
3. confirms the unit, actor boundary, period, source universe, and aggregation rule;
4. evaluates Counsel, Judgment, Command, Correction, Repair, and Reform;
5. assigns `0`, `1`, `2`, or `IE` under the published evidence-state rules;
6. evaluates institutional-record integrity and assessment-packet integrity separately;
7. records the derived overall Telemetry Integrity status;
8. identifies the Repair trigger state;
9. cites proposition-level evidence with precise locators and support relations;
10. records contradictory evidence and weighting factors;
11. completes the actor-authority matrix;
12. validates the complete assessment with the `0.5.0` conformance engine;
13. submits the locked record without discussing findings.

Structural conformance does not establish substantive correctness. A submission may pass the engine and still contain a rubric error. Disagreement analysis must retain that distinction.

## 7. Primary outcomes

### 7.1 Item-level exact agreement

For each packet, exact agreement is calculated across:

- six substantive dimension findings;
- Repair trigger state;
- institutional-record integrity status;
- assessment-packet integrity status;
- derived overall Telemetry Integrity status.

The primary packet-level proportion is:

`identical categorical outcomes / 10`

The study also reports pooled exact agreement across all packet-scorer pairs without treating the pooled value as an independent population estimate.

### 7.2 Critical disagreements

A disagreement is critical when it involves:

- `IE` versus a determinate substantive finding;
- `0` versus `2` on a substantive dimension;
- a different unit of analysis or actor boundary;
- a different Repair trigger state that changes whether Repair is scored;
- `IE` versus a determinate integrity status;
- disagreement over whether a required source was accessed;
- a schema-valid record that omits evidence required by the normative threshold;
- a contradiction that one scorer treats as dispositive and another omits.

Adjacent `0` versus `1` and `1` versus `2` findings are material. They are not automatically critical unless the disagreement reveals a boundary, evidence-state, or threshold failure.

## 8. Supplementary outcomes

The study reports these measures separately:

1. weighted ordinal agreement for `0`, `1`, and `2`, excluding `IE` from ordinal distance calculations and reporting every exclusion;
2. unweighted category agreement including `IE` as a distinct state;
3. agreement on Repair trigger state;
4. agreement on both Telemetry Integrity components and the derived overall status;
5. source-reference overlap measured by Jaccard similarity for cited source IDs and evidence proposition IDs;
6. rationale divergence classified by the disagreement taxonomy;
7. conformance outcome agreement;
8. category distribution across the three packets.

Cohen's kappa or another chance-corrected statistic may be reported only when the observed category distribution makes the statistic interpretable. The report must state when prevalence or zero variance makes the statistic unstable or undefined.

No composite HIT score is calculated.

## 9. Advancement rules

The study supports current-contract reproducibility when all conditions hold:

1. all three packets receive at least two eligible independent submissions;
2. every included submission passes schema and complete-record conformance;
3. every packet reaches at least 8 of 10 exact categorical outcomes;
4. no packet contains more than one critical disagreement;
5. the full study contains zero unresolved unit-of-analysis or actor-boundary disagreements;
6. the combined substantive findings contain at least two of the states `0`, `1`, `2`, and `IE`;
7. original submissions and pre-adjudication comparisons are preserved;
8. passing and failing packet results are published.

A Level 3 maturity decision requires the repository's separate maturity criteria in `RESEARCH.md`. Protocol passage alone does not create Level 3.

## 10. Disagreement analysis

Every nonmatching outcome is classified after original submissions are locked and the deterministic comparison is generated.

The classification must distinguish:

- source access;
- evidence selection;
- locator precision;
- proposition construction;
- support relation;
- contradictory-evidence weighting;
- evidence-state interpretation;
- ordinal threshold interpretation;
- actor or authority attribution;
- sampling or aggregation interpretation;
- Repair trigger interpretation;
- Telemetry Integrity interpretation;
- schema or conformance error;
- clerical transcription error.

The record may assign more than one cause. It must identify the crux and the contract provision that did or did not resolve it.

## 11. Adjudication

Adjudication explains disagreements and proposes contract updates. It does not overwrite an original score or alter the pre-adjudication result.

A third eligible reviewer should adjudicate disagreements when available. The author may classify process defects only when no independent adjudicator is available. That limitation must be disclosed.

The public record preserves:

- every original submission;
- scorer verification records;
- deterministic pre-adjudication comparisons;
- disagreement classifications;
- adjudication reasoning;
- proposed normative changes;
- packet-level and study-level decisions based on the frozen primary results.

## 12. Failure handling

A failing result is publishable.

The study fails its reproducibility gate when any advancement condition is unmet. The repository must publish the failed packet, preserve the original submissions, record the contract failure, and state the update condition.

No scorer may be added to replace an unfavorable result. A later replication receives a new protocol identifier and preserves the initial study.

## 13. Freeze and publication gates

Before scoring begins:

- this protocol must be merged to `main`;
- the final protocol lock must record file digests;
- all three case-selection decisions must be recorded;
- all three frozen source manifests and packet digests must exist;
- scorer-facing and coordinator-only materials must be separated;
- no author-scored result may be included in a scorer packet;
- repository validation must pass on the exact lock commit.

Release `0.7.0` may publish the locked protocol and packets. It may not publish human results that do not yet exist.
