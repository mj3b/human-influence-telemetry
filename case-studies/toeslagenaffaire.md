# Case Study 1: Dutch Childcare-Benefits Scandal

**Method:** Human Influence Telemetry 0.1.0  
**Status:** Released with repository version 0.2.0  
**Assessment:** [`assessments/toeslagenaffaire-harm-period.json`](assessments/toeslagenaffaire-harm-period.json)  
**Author:** Mark Julius Banasihan

## Assessment boundary

- **Decision:** designation of a childcare-benefit recipient as fraudulent, followed by full clawback of benefits.
- **Institutional unit:** Belastingdienst/Toeslagen.
- **Period:** 2013 through 2019, representing the documented harm architecture before the later political and remediation response.
- **Evidence boundary:** retrospective public documents, principally the Dutch parliamentary inquiry report *Ongekend Onrecht*.
- **Sampling rule:** institutional-process reconstruction. This assessment does not claim to sample or reconstruct every individual claimant file.

The response after 2020 is kept outside the harm-period findings. Combining later apology, compensation, or reform with the earlier decision process would make the original oversight architecture appear stronger than it was when decisions were being made.

## Findings

| Dimension | Finding | Public-record basis | Material record still missing |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Counsel | `0` | The inquiry reconstruction indicates that risk classification and institutional presumptions displaced case-specific evidence. | Contemporaneous file-access logs showing what reviewers opened before designation |
| Judgment | `0` | The enforcement architecture did not demonstrate individualized weighing of alternatives, proportionality, or claimant circumstances. | Individual decision rationales and documented alternatives |
| Command | `1` | Formal administrative authority remained, but the record does not show an effective capacity to direct otherwise in practice. | Reasoned orders, exercised overrides, and stop-use records |
| Correction | `1` | Objection and appeal channels existed, while the inquiry documented delay and limited practical effect. | Harm-period appeal register showing timely merits-based reversals and interruption |
| Repair | `0` | No remediation process is demonstrated during the assessed harm period. | Restitution, corrected records, notification, and named remediation ownership during the period |
| Reform | `0` | The harmful architecture continued through the assessment window. | Authorized changes to risk criteria, thresholds, workflow, or enforcement architecture |

**Dominant pattern:** signatures and formal channels survived, but evidence access, individualized judgment, effective correction, repair, and architecture change did not demonstrate practical force during the harm period.

## Telemetry Integrity

**Finding: `IE`.** The parliamentary inquiry provides a substantial external reconstruction, but it also documents incomplete and withheld records. The operating institutions controlled many of the underlying artifacts, and the public record does not establish independent, tamper-evident retention of the complete decision trail.

This is not a reason to score the six dimensions optimistically. It is a separate finding that limits confidence in what can be reconstructed.

## Rival interpretation

A reviewer could score Command `0` rather than `1` on the ground that authority that cannot be exercised is absent rather than ceremonial. This assessment uses `1` because formal authority and signatures remained observable, while their capacity to alter the decision path was not demonstrated. The disagreement should be tested in the inter-rater exercise rather than hidden.

## What this case contributes to HIT

1. It shows why later repair must not be folded into the earlier decision window.
2. It distinguishes formal authority and formal appeal from practical influence.
3. It demonstrates why Telemetry Integrity must remain separate from substantive findings.
4. It raises a scope question for Reform: whether the assessment concerns one harmed process or the wider architecture class reused elsewhere.

## Limitations

This is a documentary assessment, not a finding of legal liability, institutional intent, or causal attribution. The period boundary is conservative and does not assert that every relevant practice began or ended on an exact date. Post-2020 repair and reform require a separate assessment window.
