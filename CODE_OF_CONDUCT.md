# Code of Conduct

## Standard

Participants must engage with evidence, arguments, and implementation details without harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attack.

The project welcomes rigorous criticism. Criticism should identify the claim, mechanism, evidence, ambiguity, or failure mode at issue. Disagreement with a framework, scoring decision, institution, religious tradition, legal interpretation, or governance position does not justify hostility toward a contributor.

## Expected conduct

- distinguish factual correction from normative disagreement;
- disclose uncertainty and material conflicts of interest;
- critique artifacts and reasoning rather than personal character;
- protect confidential and personally identifying information;
- avoid presenting institutional allegations without traceable evidence;
- respect authorship, licensing, and source boundaries;
- accept correction when a claim is unsupported.

## Unacceptable conduct

- harassment, threats, stalking, or discriminatory abuse;
- doxxing or disclosure of private information;
- knowingly false attribution or fabricated evidence;
- manipulation of review, scoring, provenance, or validation records;
- repeated disruption unrelated to improving the project;
- retaliation against good-faith reporting or criticism.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the contact method listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile. The maintainer may remove content, restrict participation, reject contributions, or escalate credible threats to GitHub or appropriate authorities.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented, and separated from substantive disagreement about the research.
