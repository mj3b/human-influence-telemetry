# Invitation to Independently Score a Human Influence Telemetry Case

I am seeking two independent reviewers for a bounded research exercise involving Human Influence Telemetry (HIT), a documentary method for evaluating whether formal human oversight retained practical force in an AI-mediated institutional decision.

The task is deliberately limited:

- one institutional process;
- three public sources;
- six substantive HIT dimensions plus Telemetry Integrity;
- one structured JSON submission;
- no meeting or group deliberation required.

The exercise concerns the Cigna PxDx claims-review workflow. Reviewers receive a frozen evidence packet, the HIT specification, the application handbook, and a submission template. Reviewers work independently and do not receive the author's existing scores.

Expected effort: approximately 90 to 150 minutes, depending on reading speed and familiarity with governance evidence.

The result may pass or fail the predeclared agreement threshold. Both outcomes are publishable. Original submissions are preserved and are not rewritten after comparison.

Reviewers may use a stable public identifier instead of publishing their legal name. A short qualification summary and conflict disclosure are required.

Before accepting, please complete the eligibility screen in `eligibility-screen.md`.
