# Independent Scorer Instructions

Protocol: `HIT-IRP-CIGNA-001`  
Packet: `HIT-IR-CIGNA-PXDX-001`

## Materials you will receive

- HIT specification version 0.1.0
- HIT application handbook
- frozen assessment boundary
- frozen three-source manifest
- scorer-submission template
- scorer-submission JSON Schema
- technical validation instructions

## Process

1. Read the specification and application handbook.
2. Review all three frozen sources.
3. Confirm the assessment boundary.
4. Assign `0`, `1`, `2`, or `IE` to each substantive dimension.
5. Assign `adequate`, `limited`, `unreliable`, or `IE` to Telemetry Integrity.
6. Cite one or more frozen source IDs for every finding.
7. Explain each finding and record ambiguity.
8. Complete every independence and source-access attestation.
9. Validate the JSON submission.
10. Submit the locked file without discussing findings.

## Restrictions

Do not inspect the author's public Cigna case study or machine-readable assessment. Do not use additional case sources. Do not consult the author about evidence interpretation. Do not coordinate with another scorer.

Technical questions about file access or JSON validity are permitted. Substantive coaching is not.

## Evidence discipline

Reporting, institutional response, regulatory scrutiny, allegations, and a pleading-stage ruling have different evidentiary status. Preserve those distinctions.

Use `IE` when the packet cannot distinguish absence from missing evidence. Do not use `IE` merely to avoid an unfavorable finding. Missing public evidence alone does not prove absence.

## Preservation

Your original locked submission will remain part of the public research record. Adjudication may explain disagreement but will not overwrite your original findings.
